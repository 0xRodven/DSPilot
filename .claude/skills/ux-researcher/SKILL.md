### Interview Script

```markdown
## DSPilot User Interview Script

### Introduction (5 min)
"Hi [Name], thanks for joining. I'm [Your name] from DSPilot.

This interview will take about 30 minutes. I want to learn about how you manage driver performance - there are no right or wrong answers.

Everything you share is confidential and will only be used to improve our product.

Is it okay if I record this call? [Wait for consent]

Any questions before we start?"

---

### Warm-up (5 min)
1. "Tell me about your role. What does a typical day look like?"
2. "How long have you been managing drivers?"
3. "How many drivers do you oversee?"

---

### Current State (10 min)
4. "Walk me through how you currently track driver performance."
   - Probe: "What tools do you use?"
   - Probe: "How often do you check?"

5. "What's the most frustrating part of tracking performance?"
   - Probe: "Can you give me a specific example?"

6. "How do you identify which drivers need coaching?"
   - Probe: "What data do you look at?"

7. "Describe the last time you coached a driver. What happened?"

---

### Pain Points (5 min)
8. "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about performance tracking, what would it be?"

9. "What happens when you miss a coaching opportunity?"
   - Probe: "How does that affect your station?"

10. "How much time do you spend on performance tracking each week?"

---

### Solution Exploration (5 min)
11. "Have you tried any tools or methods to make this easier?"
    - Probe: "What worked? What didn't?"

12. "If I told you there's a tool that imports Amazon reports in 60 seconds and shows you who needs coaching, what would you want to know?"

---

### Wrap-up (5 min)
13. "Is there anything else about driver performance management you'd like to share?"

14. "Any questions for me?"

"Thank you so much for your time. Your insights are incredibly valuable."

---

### Post-Interview Notes
- Key quotes:
- Surprising findings:
- Follow-up questions:
```

### Interview Best Practices

```markdown
## DO
- Ask open-ended questions ("Tell me about..." not "Do you...")
- Use silence (let them fill the gap)
- Follow up on interesting threads
- Take verbatim notes on key quotes
- Ask for specific examples
- Stay neutral (no leading questions)

## DON'T
- Ask yes/no questions
- Suggest answers
- Talk more than 20% of the time
- Defend your product
- Promise features
- Skip the warm-up
```

## Usability Testing

### Test Plan

```markdown
## DSPilot Usability Test Plan

### Objective
Evaluate the import and coaching flow for new users.

### Participants
- 5 station managers
- Mix of tech comfort levels
- Never used DSPilot before

### Tasks

**Task 1: First Import (5 min)**
"You just signed up for DSPilot. Import this Amazon report."
- Success: Report imported, data visible
- Observe: Where they click, confusion points

**Task 2: Find Struggling Driver (3 min)**
"Find the driver with the lowest DWC score."
- Success: Navigate to driver, view details
- Observe: Navigation path, time to complete

**Task 3: Create Coaching Action (5 min)**
"Create a coaching action for this driver."
- Success: Action created with follow-up
- Observe: Form completion, field understanding

**Task 4: Check Coaching Pipeline (3 min)**
"Find all drivers who need follow-up this week."
- Success: Navigate to coaching, filter view
- Observe: Understanding of statuses

### Metrics
- Task completion rate (target: 90%)
- Time on task
- Error rate
- Satisfaction rating (1-5)

### Post-Task Questions
1. "How easy or difficult was that?" (1-5)
2. "What would you change about this?"
3. "How does this compare to your current process?"
```

### Usability Test Script

```markdown
## Introduction
"Thanks for helping us test DSPilot. I want to see how real users interact with the product - you can't do anything wrong.

Think out loud as you go. Tell me what you're looking at, what you expect, what confuses you.

I'll give you tasks but won't help unless you're completely stuck. Ready?"

---

## Task 1
"Imagine you just signed up for DSPilot. Here's an Amazon report."
[Hand over test HTML file]
"Import this report into DSPilot."

[Observe silently. Note:]
- Where they look first
- Clicks and navigation
- Verbal reactions
- Time to complete

[If stuck after 2 min:]
"What are you looking for?"
"What do you expect to happen?"

[After completion:]
"How was that? (1-5)"
"What would make it easier?"

---

## [Continue for each task...]

---

## Wrap-Up
"Overall, how would you rate the experience? (1-5)"
"What's the one thing you'd change?"
"Any final thoughts?"
```

## Research Synthesis

### Affinity Mapping

```markdown
## Affinity Mapping Process

1. **Capture**
   - Write each insight on a sticky note
   - One insight per note
   - Include participant ID

2. **Cluster**
   - Group similar notes together
   - Don't force it - let themes emerge
   - Move notes as needed

3. **Label**
   - Name each cluster
   - Use participant language when possible

4. **Prioritize**
   - Which themes appeared most?
   - Which have highest impact?
   - Which are most actionable?
```

## DO NOT
- Lead participants to desired answers
- Ignore findings that contradict hypotheses
- Test with only power users
- Skip the research plan
- Present opinions as data
- Conduct research without clear goals
